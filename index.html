Our Culture

Indian culture is a rich and diverse tapestry woven from traditions, beliefs, arts, languages, and social norms, reflecting the nation's history and its "unity in diversity" across various regions and religions.

Diversity: India is known for its vast linguistic, ethnic, and religious diversity, with numerous states and union territories each boasting unique cultural practices. Religious Diversity: Hinduism is the dominant religion, followed by Islam, Christianity, and Sikhism, among others. Values: Indian culture emphasizes respect for elders, family unity, honesty, hard work, and education. Festivals: India celebrates a wide array of festivals, each with its own traditions and significance, reflecting the diverse religious and cultural landscape. The Hawa Mahal is a palace in the city of Jaipur, Rajasthan, India. Built from red and pink sandstone, it is on the edge of the City Palace, Jaipur, and extends to the Zenana, or women's chambers.
